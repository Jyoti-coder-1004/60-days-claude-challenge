# Day 7: Claude Model Selection & Reasoning Effort

## 📋 Overview
Today's challenge focused on understanding the core fundamentals of selecting the right Claude model variant and managing its reasoning effort before jumping into prompt engineering. Selecting the ideal model based on the complexity of the task optimizes both performance and cost.

## 🛠️ Tool of the Day
* **Claude Counter:** Used to analyze tokens, evaluate input/output costs, and monitor context limits effectively.

## 🧠 Key Takeaways
1. **Model Matching:** Not every task requires the most powerful model. Simple formatting or data extraction works best on faster, lightweight models, while complex architecture and deep logic require high-tier reasoning variants.
2. **Reasoning Effort Control:** Adjusting the internal reasoning allocation helps in managing latency and execution depth, saving costs where deeper cognitive processing isn't required.
3. **Prompt Efficiency:** Pre-evaluating a task's nature saves massive amounts of development time down the line.
